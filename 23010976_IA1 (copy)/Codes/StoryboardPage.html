Storyboard Page

A storyboard and a wireframe are both planning tools used in the design process, but they serve different purposes. A storyboard is a visual representation of how a user might progress through a product, app, or website. It's like a comic strip of the user's journey, showing the sequence of screens or interactions they'll encounter. Storyboards help designers and stakeholders understand the overall flow and user experience.

A wireframe, on the other hand, is a basic visual guide that represents the skeletal framework of a website or app. It's a low-fidelity, schematic layout that outlines the specific size and placement of page elements, site features, conversion areas, and navigation. Wireframes focus on functionality and structure rather than visual design, serving as a blueprint for the final product.

For this Website in particular, I would say that the colour scheme, feel and layout is consistent throughout the entirety of the website.

Home Page Wireframe

Above is a picture of the wireframe of my Home/Index page. The general layout of this page will be simple and clear in a way that the main message to the user would be portrayed right away, without burrowing them with information they may not currently need. Being the first page a user opens, the Home/Index page is where they enter into the site's content. The idea is for it to offer an intuitive and inviting introduction.

The color scheme used will also play a very important part in making it look visually appealing. It contains a light green background that evokes a mood of serenity, freshness, and easiness as the user navigates through the site. This soft tone works well in high contrast to the brighter navigation green to create usability in the recognition of key areas of the site. Complementary shades of green make it cohesive and balanced, which controls the user's focus while keeping things clean and modern. This design balances simplicity and functionality by ensuring that users will find what they need right away with no distractions while setting the tone for the rest of the website.

Personal Development Page Wireframe

The Personal Development Page is designed to showcase my journey of growth and learning, particularly focusing on the "Twelve Pillars" by Jim Rohn and Chris Widener. The layout is structured and informative, providing a clear overview of key personal development concepts. The page is divided into sections, each representing a "Pillar" from the book. Each section includes a preview, key points, and a notable quote, making the information easily d igestible. The use of collapsible sections allows users to focus on specific areas of interest without being overwhelmed by information. This consistency in design helps maintain a sense of unity across the website. A unique feature of this page is the inclusion of personal reflections on each Pillar, demonstrating how I've applied these concepts to my life and studies in Modular Computer Studies. This personalization adds depth to the content and makes it more relatable to visitors. The page also includes information about my degree and acquired skills, providing a clear link between personal development and professional growth. This integration of personal and academic information creates a holistic view of my development journey.
