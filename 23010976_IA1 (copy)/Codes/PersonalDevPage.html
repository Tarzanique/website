"Twelve Pillars"

by Jim Rohn and Chris Widener

Pillar 4: Achieve Your Goals

This Pillar focuses on the importance of setting and achieving goals. It emphasizes the power of writing down goals and the personal growth that comes from pursuing them.

This Pillar focuses on the importance of setting and achieving goals. It emphasizes the power of writing down goals and the personal growth that comes from pursuing them. The chapter also touches on the constancy of goals amidst changing plans and introduces a method for tracking progress.

Key Points:

  • Pillar 4 is "Achieve Your Goals"
  • Writing down goals helps manifest them
  • Achieving goals leads to personal improvement
  • The process of pursuing goals is valuable in itself
  • Goals remain constant even when plans change
  • Use "Plan, Do, Check, Adjust" to track progress
"The major reason for setting a goal is for what it makes of you to accomplish it."

Pillar 5: The Proper Use of Time

This Pillar contrasts the "pain of discipline" with the "pain of regret," emphasizing the importance of taking action and not procrastinating.

This Pillar contrasts the "pain of discipline" with the "pain of regret," emphasizing the importance of taking action and not procrastinating. It stresses the value of time and making the most of each day by focusing on opportunities that align with one's goals.

Key Points:

  • The pain of discipline weighs ounces; the pain of regret weighs tons
  • Don't procrastinate; life is shorter than you think
  • Spend each day wisely, as it's a non-renewable resource
  • Focus on opportunities that align with your overall goals
  • Distinguish between urgent and important tasks
"Every day has many opportunities, but only one best opportunity."

Pillar 6: Surround Yourself With the Best People

This chapter discusses the significant impact that personal relationships and associations have on one's success and personal growth.

This chapter discusses the significant impact that personal relationships and associations have on one's success and personal growth. It advises being selective about the people you surround yourself with, emphasizing the importance of associating with successful and like-minded individuals.

Key Points:

  • Choose challenging environments for personal growth
  • Categorize relationships as positive, neutral, or negative
  • Decide whether to disassociate, limit association, or expand association with each person
  • Surround yourself with successful people who embody values you aspire to
  • Your associations significantly influence your personal development
"Don't join an easy crowd; you won't grow. Go where the expectations and the demands to perform are high."

Pillar 7: Be a Life-Long Learner

This Pillar emphasizes the importance of continuous learning beyond formal education. It highlights how self-education can lead to personal and financial success.

This Pillar emphasizes the importance of continuous learning beyond formal education. It highlights how self-education can lead to personal and financial success, and encourages readers to seek knowledge from various sources and share their learnings with others.

Key Points:

  • Most of life is lived after formal education
  • Self-education is key to personal and financial success
  • Continuously improve and grow through self-teaching
  • Learn from books, successful people, and personal experiences
  • Reflect on both successes and failures
  • Share your knowledge to help others
"Formal education will make you a living. Self education will make you a fortune."
